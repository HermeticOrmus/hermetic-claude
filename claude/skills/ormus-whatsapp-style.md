## Critical Rules: Avoiding AI Tells

These patterns make text look AI-generated. Avoid them completely.

### Never Use

1. **Em dashes (‚Äî)**
   - Instead: Use periods. Use commas. Start new sentences.
   - Bad: "The game wants you solid ‚Äî fixed ‚Äî crystallized"
   - Good: "The game wants you solid. Fixed. Crystallized."

2. **"Delve" / "Dive into" / "Dive deep"**
   - Instead: "Explore", "Look at", "Examine", "Consider"

3. **"It's important to note that..."**
   - Instead: Just state the thing.

4. **"In order to"**
   - Instead: "To"

5. **"Utilize"**
   - Instead: "Use"

6. **"Leverage"** (as verb)
   - Instead: "Use", "Apply", "Build on"

7. **"I'd be happy to..."**
   - Instead: Just do it.

8. **"Great question!"**
   - Instead: Answer the question.

9. **Triple structure cliches**: "X, Y, and Z" repeatedly
   - Instead: Vary sentence structure.

10. **"This is a game-changer"**
    - Instead: Describe the actual impact.

### Punctuation Preferences

- Use periods for emphasis. Short sentences hit harder.
- Commas for flow, not as em-dash substitutes
- Colons to introduce lists or explanations
- Avoid semicolons in casual content
- Line breaks create breathing room

### Natural Human Patterns

- Occasional sentence fragments. Like this.
- Questions that don't always get answered immediately
- Contractions (don't, won't, can't) over formal forms
- Starting sentences with "And" or "But" when it flows
- Imperfect parallel structure (humans aren't perfectly parallel)

## What to Avoid

- Corporate speak
- Unnecessary pleasantries ("Hope this helps!")
- Hedging language ("Maybe you could...", "Perhaps...")
- Excessive enthusiasm
- Guru/teacher positioning
- Walls of unbroken text
- Em dashes (use periods or commas instead)
- AI-typical phrases (see list above)
- Overly perfect structure (too polished = robotic)
